## History

Painter's Servant and Grindstone form one of the most elegant two-card combos in Magic's history. Painter's Servant names a color, causing every card everywhere to become that color in addition to its other colors. Grindstone then mills two cards at a time, but since each pair now shares a color — the one Painter named — the process chains indefinitely until the opponent's library is empty. The combo has been a Legacy fixture for years, where it also enables the one-mana hate pieces Pyroblast and Red Elemental Blast to answer any spell in the game by naming blue. With Painter's Servant now available in Timeless, the archetype has found a new home, and multiple color variants have emerged around the same core two cards.

## The Printings That Made It Possible

The infrastructure to support a two-card artifact combo has existed in Timeless for some time. Imperial Recruiter, Goblin Engineer, Tinker, and a full suite of tutors provided the search redundancy, and artifact-centric strategies were already well-developed across multiple colors. What was missing was Painter's Servant itself. Once it arrived in Timeless, those shells finally had their missing piece, and the community quickly set about testing which color provided the best home — or whether the answer was to combine several.

## White Painter

White Painter wraps the combo in a hatebears shell, using Thalia, Guardian of Thraben, Archon of Emeria, and Ethersworn Canonist to slow opponents down while the deck assembles its two pieces. Enlightened Tutor provides reliable access to both Painter's Servant and Grindstone from the same card, and Restoration Angel offers creature protection and flicker value on key pieces. The deck's defining strength is its ability to function as a legitimate disruptive creature deck even when the combo is unavailable — opponents must answer the hatebears or risk losing to them alone. In a metagame full of spell-heavy combo strategies, White Painter can be a punishing and underestimated choice.

## Blue Painter

Blue Painter leans into a control shell, using counterspells and card draw to protect the combo and dictate the pace of the game. The deck's standout interaction is Counterbalance alongside a resolved Painter's Servant: naming any color makes all cards share that color, and Counterbalance can counter any spell whose mana value matches the top card of your library — a soft lock that opponents must actively break. Tinker provides an instant-path to Grindstone at the cost of sacrificing an artifact, and Force of Will protects key pieces from disruption. Blue Painter rewards technical play and thrives in grindy games where its card selection and countermagic can dictate the pace. It is the most interactive of the Painter variants and well-suited to a controlling metagame.

## Black Painter

Black Painter operates closer to an Entomb-Reanimate shell with the Painter combo as a secondary — and often primary — win condition. Imperial Seal and Vampiric Tutor fetch both halves of the combo instantly, and Thoughtseize plus Duress clear the path through opposing interaction before the combo resolves. The deck can also use Buried Alive to set up reanimation targets alongside the combo pieces, creating multiple independent threat vectors that demand different answers. Demonic Tutor provides the most flexible tutoring available in the format, finding whatever piece is missing at any given moment. Black Painter is resilient against disruption precisely because it has enough redundancy to pivot when one plan is stopped — though graveyard hate can be problematic when leaning on the Entomb lines.

## Red Painter

Red Painter is the most aggressive variant and the one most directly descended from Legacy Painter. Imperial Recruiter tutors Painter's Servant directly — both are small creatures with the same power and toughness, making Recruiter's restriction irrelevant — and Goblin Welder recurs artifacts from the graveyard when they are destroyed. Goblin Engineer searches for Grindstone directly and can bin and recur artifacts at will. Blood Moon is a natural inclusion to lock opponents off colored mana, and with Painter naming blue, Pyroblast and Red Elemental Blast become hard counters and unconditional removal for any permanent in the game. Red Painter rewards players who understand the Pyroblast interaction deeply — it is the most punishing variant when the opponent does not respect what a resolved Painter's Servant changes about the rules of the game.

## Green Painter

Green Painter is the least established variant but offers a unique angle through raw mana acceleration. Elvish Spirit Guide, Arbor Elf, and mana dorks deploy the combo faster than any other color can manage without rituals. Natural Order can put Painter's Servant or a large creature directly into play, and Once Upon a Time provides free library filtering to find the right piece at the right time. Crop Rotation offers a free way to fetch artifact lands that feed Goblin Engineer lines, adding another layer of combo redundancy. While Green Painter's disruption suite is thinner than other colors, its speed and mana abundance can catch opponents completely unprepared. It is a legitimate combo threat capable of going off as early as turn two, and it remains an underexplored corner of the archetype.

## Closing Thoughts

Painter's Servant decks are uniquely difficult for opponents to predict, prepare for, and beat. The combo is compact, tutors are plentiful in Timeless, and a resolved Painter's Servant changes the rules of the game in ways opponents must actively account for — every Pyroblast in hand becomes live, every Counterbalance becomes a soft lock, every Grindstone becomes the end. Going forward, the deck remains a genuine wildcard: powerful enough to threaten any field, but demanding enough in sequencing and rules knowledge that pilots new to the archetype will frequently stumble.

Players looking to include maindeck answers should consider artifact removal that hits both pieces simultaneously — Collector Ouphe, Null Rod, and Shattering Spree all shut off the combo cleanly and are worth sideboard consideration at minimum. Expect Painter variants to remain a fixture of the Timeless metagame as new tutors and artifacts continue to be printed. A converged two-color build — most likely Blue-Red, combining the Pyroblast engine with countermagic and Tinker — may eventually emerge as the consensus list, but for now, the format is still deciding which color does it best.
